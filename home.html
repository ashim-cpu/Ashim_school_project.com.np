History of the Internet

The history of the internet dates back to the 1960s when ARPANET, the precursor to the modern internet, was developed by the U.S. Department of Defense. Originally designed for communication between researchers and government entities, ARPANET evolved to incorporate multiple technologies, protocols, and systems.

By the 1980s, the development of TCP/IP protocols laid the groundwork for the internet as we know it today. The World Wide Web, introduced in the early 1990s by Tim Berners-Lee, made the internet accessible to the general public, transforming communication, business, and society.
